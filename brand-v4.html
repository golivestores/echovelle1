Echovelle's craft tradition is rooted in the belief that the human hand is irreplaceable. Each piece passes through the hands of at least three master artisans — a grader who selects the pearl, a stringer who knots each strand on silk, and a setter who finishes every clasp and finding in solid 18K gold. This process cannot be rushed; a single necklace may take six hours to complete.


We honor this tradition through our Atelier Residency and Master Artisan programs.

Our Atelier Residency invites emerging and established artisans to spend six months in our workshop alongside our master graders, stringers and setters. The Master Artisan program is a three-year apprenticeship pairing each with a senior hand, passing down the quiet rituals of pearl-knotting, clasp-finishing and silk-threading that define the Echovelle house. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis; we welcome candidates in jewelry, metalsmithing, textile arts, and related fields committed to slow, considered craft.

True Beauty, Never Forced.

Three Principles

A House, Built in Three Acts.

Every decision we make begins and ends with these three commitments — to the pearl, to the hand, to the sea.

No. 01

Provenance

Every pearl is traceable to a certified farm. We visit our partners in Japan each harvest — and only the top one percent comes home with us.

No. 02

Craft

Hand-knotted silk thread. Solid 18K gold clasps. Six hours of finishing per strand. Our master stringers work the same traditional Japanese method that has defined the craft for a century.

No. 03

Conscience

We don't just take from the ocean — we give back. A portion of every purchase funds clean-ocean initiatives, and we partner exclusively with farmers committed to protecting marine ecosystems.

The Heirloom Edit

Strands designed to be passed down — hand-knotted on silk, finished in solid 18K gold, made to outlive a lifetime.

A Letter From Our Founder

I wanted a pearl necklace that felt as considered as my mother's — at a price that felt honest.

Echovelle began with a simple frustration. After years working in luxury retail, I watched women pay four figures for pearl strands that I knew, intimately, cost a fraction to make. The markups weren't for craftsmanship — they were for brand tax.

So we took a different path. We went directly to the farms that supply the biggest names in the business. We found the master stringers who spend their weekends teaching their grandchildren how to tie a silk knot without pulling a pearl off-center. And we built a house where the pearl — not the price tag — does the talking.

This is the brand I wanted when I was twenty-five, and the one I hope my daughter inherits when she's fifty. Welcome home.

Mireille Renaud Founder & Creative Director
