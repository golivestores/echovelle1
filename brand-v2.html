Our Philosophy

Welcome to the House of Echovelle, where every pearl is chosen for its quiet soul — not its shine. We craft heirloom-quality jewelry from ethically harvested pearls across the world's finest waters, guided by a single promise: to honour the ocean that grew them, the hands that shaped them, and the women who wear them for a lifetime.

At Echovelle, quiet luxury is a daily habit. Every piece is a patient answer to the question of what real beauty looks like — sourced slowly, strung by hand, and made to be inherited.

The four pillars of Echovelle

Our craft is rooted in four quiet commitments — protecting the oceans that grow our pearls, sourcing only from farms that honour slow, traditional methods, designing pieces that outlive trends, and empowering the divers, artisans and designers whose hands make our work possible. Every pearl we sell is held together by care rather than scale.

Protecting the oceans

86%

27.4%

Our key focus areas include:

  • Ocean care: We partner only with Akoya, Tahitian and South Sea farms that refuse hormones and chemical growth treatments, monitoring water quality daily to protect the ecosystems that grow each pearl.
  • Low-impact craft: Our ateliers run on certified renewable electricity, with closed-loop water recycling and zero chemical discharge.
  • Plastic-free packaging: Every piece arrives in a reusable cotton pouch and FSC-certified paper box — no foam, no plastic, no waste.
  • Blue-carbon shipping: 100% of shipping emissions are offset through mangrove and seagrass restoration in the pearl-growing regions.

Echovelle partners with four family-run pearl farms, each chosen for its slow, traditional cultivation methods:

  • Akoya (Uwajima Bay, Japan): Three-generation oyster growers producing the pearls with the world’s finest mirror lustre.
  • Tahitian (Tuamotu Atolls, French Polynesia): Lagoon farms growing naturally dark pearls in some of the cleanest waters on earth.
  • South Sea (Broome, Western Australia): Slow-grown Pinctada maxima pearls with an unusually large, satin-sheen finish.
  • Freshwater (Zhuji, China): Our design-led freshwater partner, nurturing pearls in lakes filtered by mountain springs.

From ocean to heirloom

As we grow, we ensure care is embedded across every stage of the journey — from the seawater that grows each pearl to the hands that place it around a loved one’s neck.

1
PEARL FARMING

Our pearls begin in four carefully chosen waters — Akoya bays in Japan, Tahitian lagoons, the South Sea waters of Western Australia, and Zhuji freshwater lakes. We partner exclusively with family farms that refuse growth hormones and chemical treatments, growing each pearl the slow way, over three to six years.

2
HARVEST & SORTING

Harvesting happens once a year, by hand, under the eye of a single master grader. Only 4% of harvested pearls meet Echovelle’s quality bar — judged on lustre, surface, shape, colour and nacre thickness. The rest are never dyed or treated: they go back to our farm partners.

3
ATELIER CRAFT

Every piece is designed in our Hong Kong atelier, then handed to one of 38 master artisans — most of whom have shaped pearls for 18 years or more. Settings are hand-formed from recycled 18k gold and 925 sterling silver. No casting shortcuts, no AI-assisted design.

4
PACKAGING

Each piece is nested in a reusable cotton pouch and FSC-certified paper box. Plastic-free since 2023. Even the ribbon is biodegradable cotton. Nothing goes to waste, and every detail is designed to be kept.

5
DELIVERY

Every Echovelle order ships carbon-neutral, with emissions offset through mangrove and seagrass restoration in the very seas that grew your pearl. Delivery is treated as the first day of a long relationship, not the last.

6
HEIRLOOM CARE

Every piece is built to last a lifetime and beyond. Free restringing for life, a two-year full repair warranty, and the Heirloom Program that lets a loved piece grow into its next chapter — worn by the next generation, not discarded.

Echovelle began in 2018 with a single idea: that real luxury should be quiet, slow and inheritable. Today our ateliers span Hong Kong, Japan and French Polynesia, and our pearls are worn in more than 42 countries. Every piece is designed in-house and shaped by hand — no casting shortcuts, no AI-assisted design, no compromises. Our family of artisans, divers and designers shares one commitment: to protect the waters that grow our pearls, to honour the hands that shape them, and to design pieces that will still be worn three generations from now.

True Beauty, Never Forced. Handpicked pearls, ethically sourced from the world's finest waters, crafted into timeless jewelry.
